Frothy Chop

A leading marketing application for breweries around Australia.

Overview

With an ever-increasing popularity and demand for independent craft style breweries in Australia, Creative Protocol proposes to develop an application for mobile devices that will allow easy and efficient map and information access to explore local listed breweries. The App would be designed to work on iOS and Android devices and would also include website application capabilities. The Application would rely on GPS technology to gather and pin information, allow radius searching of local breweries and venues and to search for specific breweries. A review rating system, push notifications, digital calendar for logging and tracking events and simple feedback system will be incorporated to increase user interface engagement. We will be filling a market gap for those who are time poor and want efficient triangulated access to their local beer creators. 

With significant demand for a product such as Frothy-Chop and a lack of market presence of Applications that are not limited to web-based design we are assured of the success and viability of our product.

The App is designed with an easy-to-use UI implementing the drop-down menus, free scrolling to different areas and GPS location triangulation. Upon finding an area, users can click on the icon and see the name and rating of a location and tap again to open to see menus, reviews, and events. There is also an easy to access taskbar, found on the top left, this provides more menu items such as a wish list for brews you would like to try or buy, events tab for any events that may be currently happening. This menu will also include a Feedback and Help section) so should any part of the application not be satisfactory; they can let us know and we can implement changes accordingly. 

We believe the structure behind this app will be easily transferred into similar projects like a version for coffee shops, wineries, and gin distilleries. A lack of availability and an increased demand demonstrate viability of the proposed application of Creative Protocol.


Motivation

There have been significant increases in the number of Independent Breweries within Australia (99 in New South Wales out of 294 nationally) (Statista Nov. 2020), resulting in a lack of consolidated brewery information, reviews, and pinned locations available for easy reference by craft beer connoisseurs. Also, there has been a significant market shift in recent years with increasing number of micro-pub style breweries being created due to lack of shelf space in major retailers, (Sutton, 2021). Creative Protocol propose to create a mobile phone application and website that will help customers discover new breweries and their products, and plan for any trips to the cellar door using our brewery catalogue. Current market analysis indicates that there is a gap in the market for a tool with the level of functionality included, with an existing website (with no associated mobile app) identified without key proposed features included.

There are other apps and websites available that offer similar user information; however, we are aiming to provide our users a point of difference. These competitor applications are lacking the depth of scope we are trying to provide our users, lacking several important and sought-after features, and easy to navigate user experience. Some points of difference include: 

• Attractive and easy to navigate ‘clean’ User Interface
• App based Application with GPS map capability
• Quick Guide – ‘walkthrough’ for new users through features and interface capability
• Quick review/rating system for both brewers and App development suggestions
• Available on iOS and Android
• Funded and database populated by IBA 

One of our biggest competitors, Crafty Pint, was initially crated as an online magazine. They have managed to acquire breweries to sign up through the popularity of their website as a review base for breweries and have now created a limited web-based platform for viewing locally reviewed breweries. We intend to use a combination of methods to populate the app with brewery information, accessing backing from IBA and populating from their established database, using data that is publicly available and then reaching out to the individual breweries and request more specific information like what drinks they have on tap, upcoming events, and upcoming specialty releases.

Our user submitted reviews of the beers available at each brewery and the ability to build individual profiles of users based off ratings to recommend breweries and specific brews that users with a similar profile also rated highly is a point of difference in our application that is unseen in our competitors. Our competitors also do not offer any option to search for specific brews, only to search breweries and then view the catalogue. We believe this is a fundamental flaw in our competitors apps due to the belief that users not only want to visit different breweries but may also want to search for a particular brew and find out where it is available. 

Unlike our competitors our date will be sourced primarily from IBA and will therefore be the most comprehensive list of breweries while also allowing listed brewers to add details and updates to their listings. We will provide incentive for breweries to deliver information to us when it is relevant by publishing content that attracts users to the app. The content will include at home craft brewing tips, articles written about newly released brews and upcoming breweries (items that are consistently missing and asked for on our competitors’ products). Development & Backend

Firstly, like nearly every mobile app, Frothy Chop needs a backend service. With any of the 3 major players (AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google) services you can rapidly build backend services that can scale to meet demand, synchronize data across devices and send notifications. As discussed briefly in assignment two, we believe AWS Amplify is an attractive idea as this is going to help us in creating a backend and other components without needing the extensive expertise to create one from scratch. This will help to centralise all the app components within a manageable console, the Amplify components will make it easier to set up the building blocks of our app without the need for outsourcing as many specific skills that would be costly, also we will only be paying for what services we use, this will prevent us from spending investment money on hardware that isn’t going to be utilized. We plan to use a hosted cloud database to store account information, reviews and feedback of our users, extremely popular amongst the biggest tech companies in the world, an open-source software known as PostgreSQL is a standout, it allows huge customization for developers, has extensive capacity and is highly trusted for its data integrity. Keeping in uniform with the previously mentioned feature, the AWS Amazon Relational Databases will make it easy to set up, operate and scale PostgreSQL deployments in the cloud. Amazon RDS will manage time consuming tasks such as the PostgreSQL software upgrades, storage management and data backups for disaster recovery, whilst still allowing access to all the capabilities of the PostgreSQL database engine that is so familiar with professionals that we may have to outsource to. The core functionality of the app relies on data sourced from the IBA, to provide information on breweries, their products and any events or specials that they may be running. This data will be queried and synchronised with Frothy Chop’s backend database using IBA’s Application Programming Interface (API). Data queries that by their nature require more immediate and live responses than the window of database synchronisation allows will be performed against IBA’s API directly, with queries coming from the app traversing an API gateway or Enterprise Integration Platform (EIP) hosted as part of Frothy Chop’s backend. 

The use of APIs to exchange data between the IBA and Frothy Chop provides a clear demarcation between IBA’s and Frothy Chop’s internal infrastructure. Queries against the API limit the type of transactions allowed between external entities to those that are intended for 3rd party access and provide a layer of security between both entities systems, while the use of an Enterprise Integration Platform enables the app to be able to communicate via the EIP to multiple different entities without needing to know the specifics about that entity’s API. 

The decision to transfer the data from IBA into Frothy Chop’s backend and keep it synchronised and the use of a hosted API gateway or EIP instead of the app querying IBA’s API directly will allow further breweries not associated with the IBA or other industries to be integrated into the app with minimal effort, and to ensure that the app can continue to function should IBA suffer an outage with their infrastructure.

Security

Cyber security is of utmost importance to us and our team. On top of any obligations that may be applicable under the Privacy Act 1988 in Australia, serious damage to Creative Protocol’s reputation can occur if a data breach impacting users’ Personally Identifiable Information (PII) happens, or if Creative Protocol’s response to any such breach is not considered appropriate by the users. All reasonable measures will be taken to ensure the security of the environment and data held within the system. 

Communication between the Frothy Chop app and the backend hosted in AWS will be secured with Transport Layer Security (TLS), which provides assurance to the client app that the server it’s connecting to is who it says it is, as well as encrypting the traffic between the app and the server to ensure information cannot be seen by unauthorised parties or manipulated. The use of public Wi-Fi by end users, such as is present in a lot of hospitality venues, introduces risks to data security and integrity of communications from interception and rogue access points. Creative Protocol will make use of certificate pinning within the app, a practice that ensures the server’s certificate is the one expected, to ensure that interception, or man-in-the-middle, attacks are prevented. 

The app’s supporting infrastructure hosted within AWS by necessity will be Internet-facing and susceptible to data breach attempts. The use of cloud native constructs for the traditional security roles of Web Application Firewall (WAF) and Intrusion Prevention System (IPS), such as Amazon API Gateway, AWS Network Firewall, AWS WAF and Amazon GuardDuty, will provide a layer of security in front of the front-end supporting servers while being able to scale with traffic load resulting from the increasing number of users. 

Penetration testing, also known as ethical hacking, can provide significant benefits to businesses in assessing their Information Technology environments and the computer software they run (Goel & Mehtre, 2015). Although it is practically impossible to have 100% vulnerability free systems, penetration testing can identify known issues with environment configuration and running software, providing businesses an opportunity to remove as many vulnerabilities as possible prior to a system going live and becoming Internet accessible (Goel & Mehtre, 2015). Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing has a total 9 step process, being Scope, Reconnaissance, Vulnerability Detection, Information Analysis and Planning, Penetration Testing, Privilege Escalation, Result Analysis, Reporting and Clean Up. There are several forms of testing which can include manual testing, automated testing and fuzz testing. Penetration testing tools that Creative Protocol could possibly use are Metasploit, Nessus or Burp Suite, although it is recommended to engage a third-party specialist to perform the testing to provide a “fresh eyes” audit of the environment. 

Whilst Cyber Security within Information Technology can be an overwhelming topic, Creative Protocol aims to use tools that are user friendly and low impact, but also functional and effective at protecting our data and computer systems. Creative Protocol is committed to ensuring that our data and that of our end users remains safe and away from unauthorised eyes.

Testing

Creative Protocol take testing environments seriously and we aim to have a fully working application upon its release to the public. There are multiple ways of testing an application and we must consider multiple differences between devices such as screen size, operating systems and mobile network operators. It is important for the testing to be an on-going task as it will help us to have a better understanding of how the app is performing as we move through different stages of development. Testing mobile applications is more challenging than testing web apps on a desktop and creative protocol aim to mitigate these issues with mobile testing such as network variability (WIFI/3G/4G), phone performance with simultaneous application usage, battery consumption issues and memory leakage none of which are issues with testing desktop applications. Our initial testing phase will mitigate these issues using standardised hardware and testing environments before a testing rollout to broader environments is carried out. 

Usability testing is to be done by real-life users rather than the development team as they may miss defects that are exclusively related to the user experience. Feedback on our application is a significant tool we plan to use in the development phase of our application. We believe it is imperative to get real, honest feedback as early in the development cycle as possible. We have planned the first step towards this as explorative usability testing with a working app prototype at its most fundamental level. 

We believe that the earlier this initial testing phase is commenced the more efficient our application development will be as we will have the ability to create targeted development plans and timeframe them accordingly. This early testing phase we will also be of importance as we tailor and develop the overall design of the product. We believe the most cost-effective way to produce this feedback is to give selected people without prior knowledge (whilst still in the target audience) access to our app. Known as hallmark testing, this way we can obtain more honest feedback for improvement if needed. We will only need 5 test users to highlight 75%-99% of usability issues. To find these users within our target market will be a relatively simple exercise as we will find our test users in the location, we would expect our app to be used, Breweries. 

Testing Parameters

Compatibility Testing
Creative Protocol will need to ensure that our app is going to work across different devices, browsers, screen sizes and OS versions 

Interface Testing
Creative Protocol will test all features of the interface, including buttons, menu options, settings, wish list and the general flow of the navigation on the application. 

Performance Testing
Performance of the application on multiple devices and how is our app going to perform under different circumstances regarding connectivity (3G, 4G, 5G, Wi-Fi) Also to note in this area will be battery consumption. 

Security Testing
We believe that security testing to ensure our app is protecting our user’s data is necessary even in initial phases of development. 

Towards the end of the development cycle, Creative Protocol believe that an expert review is desirable. Although this will be and expensive budget cost this will provide peer feedback through testing the usability of the test application, providing feedback, and submitting results. The results of expert peer review testing will be an indicating factor when we will be ready to launch to the public. 

We will also test the application ourselves on platforms such as Browserstack on an ongoing basis to ensure functionality and development. We believe it is important to test our mobile app on a real device cloud that offers an extensive range of Android and iOS mobile devices allowing simulation of how it is/will be working in real-world conditions. 


Timeframe

Creative Protocol has estimated with the best of their knowledge a projected timeframe for the extended period of 10 more allocated weeks of project development. The team at Creative Protocol plan to implement a strategy centred around design thinking (DT) in which we will utilise for the further 10 weeks of allocated project time. DT has been recognised as a methodology that promotes the design of engaging and user-friendly apps (Shahrasbi, Jin and Zheng 2021). With a further 10 weeks of project development, we believe that by implementing such a strategy, the team would present the best opportunity for the project’s success. According to the UK Design Council (UK Design Council 2010), DT is a method which understands problems and offers innovative solutions. 

DT has 5 main phases: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test. 

Empathize – The first phase for the team is the empathize phase. This phase will allow the team to commence consumer surveying across multiple brewery locations to gain an insight into our users needs, behaviours, attitudes and experiences within a brewery setting. We feel that surveying can be approached in multiple forms such as, face to face interviewing which involves groundwork between brewery locations, we also believe that surveying can be completed through a more quantitative approach by gathering user data through traditional pen and paper surveying. The latter approach may be executed through solid communication and building relationships with local breweries as to gain permission to have our survey papers displayed within their brewery for patrons to fill out. 

Define – After conducting our user research we aim to define who our targeted audience is and what their needs are. This stage involves the commitment of analysing numerous amounts of data to understand the mean of our statistics received and to understand our users desires on a deeper level. 

Ideate – With the collection of data received and a thorough analysis, Creative Protocol will initialise the ideate phase of the DT strategy. The phase will highlight the importance of teamwork as we together build our understanding of user needs and begin to implement feature ideas for the app. 

Prototype – In the prototype phase the team will begin to build out a low fidelity prototype. Creative Protocol will design the app on paper through hand sketches and Microsoft PowerPoint. This lowfidelity prototype will be used amongst the group to test the useability and to gain feedback on its performance within the team before testing is taken further. After developing a low-fidelity prototype, the team will commence the building of a high-fidelity prototype. The team plan to use such applications as Figma (Figma 2022). 

Test – Now the team has developed a low-fidelity prototype, and a high-fidelity prototype, the team can then take these developments into the testing phase. The testing phase involves numerous user testing within the team group, our targeted users, and any other outside parties which could provide professional and technical feedback such as fellow students, university tutors and lecturers and industry professionals.

We have created a projected timeframe for our project should we be able to bring our project through to development and completion.



Risks

Understanding that all projects contain risks both known, and unknown is paramount to a successful outcome. By implementing risk management strategies as a team, we believe we can deliver the project in all aspects of success. To do this, we must identify the potential risks involved in building the application. 

Our app will provide the end user with a range of features, from guiding them to a local brewery, rating their experience, receiving product updates and push notifications. As we implement features, we run the risks of trying to provide too much for the end user. A common practice in app development is to create an app which contains to many features and not enough purpose for the end user (Chanchal 2019). Building an app is all about the end users experience, so we must be sure to identify our end users’ needs correctly. 

Another area of potential downfall for the project is in our own expectations of the app’s success. How sure are we that our app will be successful? We have identified other companies who provide the app with similarities, and we strive to create something better than currently offered. In attempting to recreate the industry benchmark, we may fail in our attempts to deliver a better application which may not necessarily be in demand from market participants. If we stretch the boundaries too far in the development of a new and improved brewery app, we may underestimate our markets requirements and venture into scope creep. 

App development requires many programming skills and resources to produce a well-functioning user-friendly application. As we undertake the development of the app, it is necessary for us as a group to identify the required skillset needed to achieve a successful development. Effective communication throughout the project’s life cycle is of high importance to assist the team in understanding the depth of software development skills required to complete our project. We must understand that our skillset may not be at the level required to fulfil the projects development requirements within the designated timeframe.
